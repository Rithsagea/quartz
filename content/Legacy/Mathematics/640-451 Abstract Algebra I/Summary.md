# Definitions
### Group Theory
<u>Group</u> a set of elements $G$ equipped with a binary operation satisfying
- *associativity* $a(bc)=(ab)c$
- *identity* $a1=1a=a$
- *inverse* $aa^{-1}=1$

<u>Subgroup</u> $H<G$ if $H\subset G$ with closure, identity, and inverse

<u>Abelian Group</u> a group that is commutative, i.e. $ab=ba$
<u>Cyclic Group</u> $\langle x\rangle$ a group generated by a single element $x$
<u>Normal Subgroup</u> $N\lhd G$ if $\forall a\in N$, $\forall g\in g$, the conjugate $gag^{-1}\in N$
<u>Simple Group</u> a group with no proper normal subgroups
<u>Commutator Subgroup</u> $[A,B]=\{aba^{-1}b^{-1}|a\in A,b\in B\}$

<u>Product Group</u> $G\times G'=\{(g,g')|g\in G,g'\in G'\}$ with inherited binary operation
<u>Quotient Group</u> $G/N$ group of cosets of $N\lhd G$

<u>Order</u> $|G|$, number of elements of a group; smallest number $k$ s.t. $x^k=1$

<u>Homomorphism</u> $\varphi:G\rightarrow G'$ where $\varphi(ab)=\varphi(a)\varphi(b)$
<u>Isomorphism</u> a bijective homomorphism
<u>Automorphism</u> an isomorphism from a group onto itself

<u>Kernel</u> $\ker\varphi=\{a\in G|\varphi(a)=1\}$
<u>Left Coset</u> $H<G$, $a\in G$, $aH=\{ah|h\in H\}$
<u>Index</u> $|G:H|$ for $H>G$, the number of cosets of $H$ contained in $G$

### Group Actions

<u>Group Action</u> $G\times S\rightarrow S$ given a group $G$ and set $S$, where
- $(1\in G)(\forall s\in S)\ 1*s=s$
- $(\forall g,g'\in G)(\forall s\in S)\ (gg')s=g(g's)$

<u>Orbit</u> $G$ acting on $S$, $s\in S$, $O_s=\{gs|g\in G\}$
<u>Stabilizer</u> $G$ acting on $S$, $s\in S$, $G_s=\{g\in G|gs=s\}$

<u>Transitive</u> An action with only one orbit
<u>Faithful</u> An action where $gs=s$ IFF $g=1$

<u>Centralizer</u> $Z(x)=\{g\in G|gxg^{-1}=x\}$
<u>Conjugacy Class</u> $C(x)=\{gxg^{-1}|g\in G\}$
<u>Normalizer</u> $N(H)=\{g\in G|gHg^{-1}=H\}$

<u>p-group</u> A group where $|G|=p^k$ for some prime $p$

### Representation Theory

<u>Complex Representation</u> a homomorphism $\rho:G\rightarrow GL(V)$

<u>Character</u> trace of a representation $\chi=\operatorname{Tr}\circ\rho$
<u>Inner product</u> $\langle\chi,\chi'\rangle=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_g\overline{\chi(g)}\chi'(g)=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{C_k}|C_k|\overline{\chi(c_k)}\chi'(c_k)$

<u>Dimension</u> the dimension of the codomain of $\rho$, or $\chi(1)$
<u>G-invariant</u> a vector or space fixed by $\rho_g$ for all $g\in G$; a linear operator $T$ compatible with $\rho$, $T(\rho_gv)=\rho_gT(v)$
<u>Isomorphic</u> two irreps $\rho:G\rightarrow GL(V)$ and $\rho':G\rightarrow GL(V')$ are isomorphic if there is a G-invariant linear map $T:V\rightarrow V'$, $T\rho_gv=\rho'_gTv$
<u>Irreducible</u> A representation with no G-invariant subspaces

<u>Permutation Representation</u> Let $G$ act on $S$, and $R_g$ be the permutation matrix. This is a representation of a vector space with basis $S$.
<u>Regular Representation</u> $\rho^{reg}$ is the permutation representation of $G$ acting on itself by left multiplication

# Useful Tools
<u>Chinese Remainder Theorem</u> For $N=\prod_k n_k\in\mathbb N$, where $n_k$ are pairwise coprime, $\mathbb Z/N\mathbb Z\cong\prod_i\mathbb Z/n_i\mathbb Z$

<u>Lagrange's Theorem</u> For $H<G$, $|H|$ divides $|G|$

<u>Correspondence Theorem</u> Let $\varphi:G\rightarrow\mathcal G$ be a surjective homomorphism with kernel $K$. Then $\varphi$ is a bijection between subgroups of $\mathcal G$ and subgroups of $G$ containing $K$.

<u>Direct Product Theorem</u> Let $H,K<G$, and $f:(h,k)\mapsto hk$.
- $f$ is injective IFF $H\cap K=\{1\}$
- $f$ is a homomorphism IFF for all $h\in H$, $k\in K$, $k=kh$
- If $H$ normal, then $HK<G$
- $f$ is an isomorphism IFF $H\cap K=\{1\}$, $HK=G$, and $H,K\lhd G$

<u>First Isomorphism Theorem</u> Let $\varphi:G\rightarrow G'$ be surjective with kernel $N$. $\overline G=G/N$ is isomorphic to $G'$, and there is a unique isomorphism $\overline\varphi:\overline G\rightarrow G'$ s.t. $\varphi=\overline\varphi\circ\pi$, where $\pi$ is the canonical quotient map.

<u>Orbit Stabilizer Theorem</u> Let $G$ act on $S$ and denote $H=G_s$. For $s\in S$, there is a bijection $\epsilon:G/H\rightarrow O_s$ with $[aH]\leadsto as$

<u>Counting Formula</u> $|S|=|O_1|+|O_2|+\cdots+|O_k|$

<u>Class Equation</u> $|G|=|C_1|+|C_2|+\cdots+|C_k|$

<u>Conjugating Permutations</u> For $\sigma,(x_1x_2\cdots)\in S_n$, $\sigma(x_1x_2\cdots)\sigma^{-1}=(\sigma(x_1)\sigma(x_2)\cdots)$

<u>First Sylow Theorem</u> A finite group $G$ with order divisible by prime $p$ has a Sylow p-subgroup

<u>Second Sylow Theorem</u> Let $G$ be a finite group with order divisible by prime $p$
- The Sylow p-subgroups are conjugate subgroups
- Every subgroup of $G$ that is a $p$-subgroup is contained in a Sylow p-subgroup

<u>Third Sylow Theorem</u> Let $G$ be a finite group with order divisible by prime $p$. $|G|=p^em$ and $n_p$ is the number of Sylow p-subgroups. Then $n_p$ divides $m$ and $n_p\equiv 1\pmod p$

<u>Classification of Finite Abelian Groups</u> Every finite abelian group is isomorphic to direct product of cyclic groups. These are isomorphic up to factorization $\prod_iC_{p_i^{k_i}}$ by Chinese Remainder Theorem

<u>Standard Representation</u> For a finite rotation group, this is the representation on $\mathbb C^3$, where $\rho_g$ represents rotations on this space

<u>Maschke's Theorem</u> Every finite representation $G$ on a non-zero, finite-dimensional complex vector space is the direct sum of irreducible representations.

<u>Main Theorem of Characters</u> Let $G$ be a finite group
- The irreducible characters of $G$ are orthonormal
- There are as many isomorphism classes of irreps as conjugacy classes of the group
- Let $\rho_1,\cdots,\rho_r$ be the irreps of $G$, and $\chi_1,\cdots,\chi_r$ their characters. Then $d_i\equiv\chi_i(1)$ divides $|G|$ and $|G|=d_1^2+\cdots+d_r^2$

<u>Characters of Finite Abelian Groups</u> Let $G$ be a finite abelian group
- Every irrep of $G$ has dimension 1. The number of irreps is $|G|$
- Every matrix representation of $G$ is diagonalizable

<u>Schur's Lemma</u>
- Let $\rho$ and $\rho'$ be irreps of $G$ to $V$ and $V'$. Let $T:V'\rightarrow V$ be G-invariant. $T$ is an isomorphism, or $T=0$
- Let $\rho$ be an irrep of $G$ on $V$, and let $T:V\rightarrow V$ be G-invariant. Then $T$ is multiplication by a scalar, $c\mathbb I$

<u>Useful Groups</u>
- $S_n$ symmetric group *permutations*
- $A_n$ alternating group *even permutations*
- $C_n$ cyclic groups *symmetries of n-gon w/o inversions*
- $D_n$ dihedral groups *symmetries of n-gon with inversions*
- $K_4\cong C_2\times C_2$ Klein 4 group *smallest non-cyclic simple group*
- $Q_8$ quaternion group $i^2=j^2=k^2=-1$, $ij=k$, $ji=-k$
- $T\cong A_4$ tetrahderal group *symmetries of tetrahedron w/o inversions*
- $O\cong S_4$ octahedral group *symmetries of octahedron w/o inversions*
- $I\cong A_5$ icosahedral group *symmetries of icosahedron w/o inversions*
- $GL_n(\mathbb F)$ invertible $n\times n$ matrices over a field $\mathbb F$
- $GL(V)$ invertible matrices over a vector space $V$
- $SL_n(\mathbb F)$ $n\times n$ determinant 1 matrices over a field $\mathbb F$
- $Z_n\equiv\mathbb Z/n\mathbb Z$ additive group of integers modulo $n$
- $Z_p^*\cong C_{p-1}$ multiplicative group of integers modulo prime $p$ 

# Magic Tricks
### Maps
- A homomorphism $\varphi$ is injective IFF $\ker\varphi=\{1\}$
- The kernel of a homomorphism is a normal subgroup
- There is a homomorphism $\varphi:S_4\rightarrow S_3$, where $S_4$ permutes elements in partitions of $\{1,2,3,4\}$, while $S_3$ permutes these partitions.
- Left multiplication and conjugation are automorphisms
- Inversion is an automorphism IFF the group is abelian
- If $G$ is simple, then every homomorphism has kernel $G$ or $\{1\}$
- If $x$ is prime, then $Z_x^*\cong C_{x-1}$ (multiplication group $\bmod x$)

### Groups
- If $|G|$ is prime, then $G$ is cyclic
- The order of $g\in G$ divides $|G|$
- Work with groups, subgroups, or cosets instead of element chasing
	- $AB=\{ab|a\in A,b\in B\}$
	- $A^{-1}=\{a^{-1}|a\in A\}$
- $H\lhd G$, then for $a\in G$, $aH=Ha$
- $|G:H|=2$, then $H\lhd G$

### More Groups
- Abuse counting formula
	- $|G|/|H|=|G:H|=|G/H|$
	- $|G|=|G_s||O_s|=|Z(g)||C(g)|$
- Let $H<G$ and $N\equiv N(H)$. $H\lhd N\le G$ and $H\lhd G$ IFF $N=G$
- $[A,B]=\{1\}$ iff the elements of $A$ commute with the elements of $B$
- $A,B\lhd G$ then the commutator $(aba^{-1})b^{-1}\in B$, $a(ba^{-1}b^{-1})\in A$

### Isomorphism Classes
- Compare group order
- Compare centralizer $Z(G)$
- Apply product theorem (<u>only for proof, not disproof</u>)

### Group Actions
- Consider group acting on itself, or its cosets by conjugation or left multiplication
- Construct homomorphism from aforementioned group action to $S_n$
- Look at orbits or stabilizers of elements.

### Sylow
- If a group has only one Sylow-p subgroup, that subgroup is normal
- Do casework on normality
- The center of a $p$-group is nontrivial
- $|G|=p^2$ implies $G$ is abelian
- $G/Z(G)$ cyclic implies $G$ is abelian
- For $n\ge 5$, $A_n$ is a simple group
- Let $H$ be a Sylow $p$-subgroup. Then the index of $N(H)$ is $n_p$
- Consider group acting on subgroup by conjugation to determine commutativity

### Rep Theory
- For $g\in[G:G]$, $\chi(g)=1$ for all $\dim 1$ irreps of $G$.
- For $g\in Z(G)$, $|\chi(g)|=\chi(1)$ for all irreps of $G$.
- For any irrep $\rho$, $\rho(\ker \rho)=\rho(1)$
- If $N\lhd G$, then for $\rho$ irrep of $G/N$, $\rho\circ\pi$ is an irrep of $G$.
- Quotient out $N\lhd G$ and compute char table of $G/N$.
	- Reindex group summation $h\sum_g\rho_g=\sum_{hg}\rho_{hg}=\sum_{g'}\rho_{g'}$
- Average linear transform $\tilde T=\frac{1}{|G|}\sum_{g\in G}\rho^{-1}_gT\rho'_g$. If $T$ is G-invariant, then $\tilde T=T$
- The columns and rows of a character table are orthogonal
- Scaling the columns of a character table by $\sqrt{\frac{|C_k|}{|G|}}$ yields a unitary matrix
- The character of a permutation matrix is the number of elements it fixes.
- Take inner product of a character with itself, or other irreps to find its composition
	- $\langle\chi,\chi\rangle=1$ IFF $\chi$ is irreducible
	- $\langle\chi,\chi\rangle=2$ IFF $\chi$ is sum of two irreducible characters
	- $\langle\chi,\chi\rangle=3$ IFF $\chi$ is sum of three irreducible characters
	- $\langle\chi,\chi\rangle=4$ IFF $\chi$ is sum of four irreducible characters or two copies of one irreducible character
- $\rho^{reg}\cong d_1\rho_1\oplus\cdots\oplus d_r\rho_r$, where $d_i$ is dimension of the irrep $\rho_i$
- Square group summation and reindex
- Apply trace to averaged function
